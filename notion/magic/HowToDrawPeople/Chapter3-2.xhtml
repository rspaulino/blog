Head Shapes

You can see how the heads on this page are more defined versions of those in the Head and Neck demonstration (page 23). They still have the cranium in back and the face attached at the front, but they are more cohesive forms. Instead of just a line indicating the eye line, a wedge shape has been cut out for the brow and eye region. The mouth is built on top of a saucer shape attached to the face. The jawline has a distinct edge and clearly shows thickness. Drawing the head this way uses the exact same skills you practiced on the previous page.
