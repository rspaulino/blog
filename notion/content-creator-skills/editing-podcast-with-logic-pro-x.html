Non-destructive Editing
Non-Destructive Editing means any edits you make won’t permanently change your audio files
The audio regions in your sessions aren’t actually Audio Files. Instead, they refer to the Audio Files in your project.
Audio Regions are like photocopies. You can cut a photocopy, fold it, or burn it if you’d like. But the original document remains unchanged.
That’s the beauty of Audio Regions. You can trim the ends, add fades, or bump up the Gain. But the original Audio File itself isn’t affected.
If you decide to reuse the Audio File in a different session, you start from a clean slate.
That’s why we call 99% of editing in Logic Non-Destructive. Because the edits you make won’t change the original file.

Fade Tool
Drag, Shuffle, Delete & Move
With Our current workflow, we select the area we want to remove, press delete and drag the region to close the gap. But it wouldn't be great if the region automatically move itself to fill the gap? because that's what we want to happen here.
What we could do is to change the Drag Mode from X-Fade to Shuffle L. So now when we delete our marquee selection our region will automatically fill the gap.
Now the only problem with this is if you have the drag mode set to shuffle L then when you moving region around the track area to the Left then they going to snap, shuffle to the next region.
You can override that Drag mode so when you moving region across the track area they don't snap, shuffle. That's one way but I find it a little bit clumsy.
So let's put the Drag mode back to X-Fade.
Another way is once you delete the selection, select the region and press the shuffle button in the toolbar.
BUT there is an even better way to do this. Instead of pressing Delete, we can Delete and Move.
With Delete and Move the selected area disappear, the new region is move to fill the gap AND any following region will also move up the same amount of time as the deleted marquee selection which is brilliant for keeping audio gap between your region intact.
Now let set a new shortcut for Delete and Move to make it even quicker.
