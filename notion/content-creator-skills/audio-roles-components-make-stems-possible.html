Audio Roles: Components Make Stems Possible
Role Components Make Stems Possible
Assigning roles and subroles
An audio mix is composed of three principle elements, or audio stems: dialog, effects and music. The levels of these stems are adjusted such that when they are combined in the Timeline, they create the final mix without requiring further level adjustment.
Here’s the project for this article. Lots of dialog, with multiple split edits, as well as music and effects clips.
First, we need to get these clips organized. Select all the clips that are the same, for example, dialog, then right-click one of the selected clips.
This allows you to set the Audio or Video Role for all the selected clips. Here, for example, I’m applying the Dialogue Role. The other dialogs are subroles, which can be used for other languages, more refined classifications, or just ignored.
Here, I’m assigning the Music Role to the music clip by right-clicking on the selected clip.
When we do, two things happen:
The clip changes color, reflecting its changed Role.
The clip changes position, because clips of one Role can’t be in the middle of clips of a different Role.
Assigning the Effects Role to the two effects clips yields the results see here; a color change and all effects clips grouped together.
CREATING STEMS FROM ROLE WITH COMPOUND CLIP COMPONENTS
Select your entire project (or that portion that you want to create stems for)
Name the compound clip something that makes sense to you.
Open the Timeline Index → Roles button → Show Audio Lanes button
Instantly, the audio stems are displayed as Role Components! These allow us to adjust audio levels for each stem, without affecting other stems.
If you need to adjust each clip individually, you don’t need to break apart the compound clip, which would destroy all your effects. Instead, double-click the compound clip to “step into it,” to access each of the clips.
Then, click the “Go Back” button next to the compound clip name at the top of the Timeline to “step back out” of the compound clip and into your project.
