The major gap in Paul’s resume is specifics. He’ll assert something such as “brought new products to market,” but it’s not really clear which projects those are. Similarly, he’ll say that he “increased the productivity of development team,” but he doesn’t explain exactly how he did that.

There’s another issue that’s more minor but still worth correcting: formatting. He’s bolded the job titles as though to say, “Hey! Look at me! I’m a product manager!” This isn’t a particularly interesting detail; many job applicants will be product managers. Instead, he should highlight his companies.

The “areas of expertise” section won’t hurt Paul, but it also won’t help much either. It’s better to demonstrate these skills through accomplishments.

Some projects or activities outside of work would also be an improvement. If he has these, he should list them.
