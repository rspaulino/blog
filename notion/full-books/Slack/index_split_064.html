A Little Sleight of Hand in the Accounting Department

 

THERE IS NO LEAVING the subject of overtime without mentioning a minor case of fraud in the way we account for these extra hours in most organizations. Knowledge workers are usually not paid for overtime hours. What they are paid for is, typically, forty hours a week. A worker who has put in sixty hours in a given week now goes through a little ceremony at the end of that week, filling out some sort of a time record that reports Total Hours Worked as forty. He/she has been assured that management is well aware of the extra hours heroically contributed to the cause, but the time sheet (“really just something to keep Personnel off our backs”) has to report a pro forma forty-hour week.
