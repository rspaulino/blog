Safety Again

 

I stressed earlier that the people who will be asked to bear the brunt of the change have to feel safe. This applies equally well to the people who conceive of the change and direct its implementation. Change can’t happen without risk, and risk-taking is only possible in an environment that can be tolerant of at least some failure. There is a paradox at work here: Making it okay to take risks and succeed in risky endeavors requires that you also make it okay to fail.

If you’re having trouble with the notion that risk-taking requires tolerance for failure, consider the opposite: punishment for failure. Potential punishment is often justified as a way to assure success; it is more often a deterrent to taking any risks at all.
