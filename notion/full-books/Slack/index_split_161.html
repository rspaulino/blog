Risk Management: The Minimal Prescription

 

A RISK is a bad thing that might happen or might not (or it might happen to some degree). It’s bad because should it happen, your chances of overall success are lessened. When the bad thing happens, the risk is said to materialize.

My simple definition of risk management was explicit declaration of uncertainty about the effect that expected materializations may have, and that is certainly the key element. But in addition there is a bit of homework to be done for each identified uncertainty. Work you need to do on a per-risk basis is the subject of this chapter. There are two classes of risk that are handled somewhat differently: aggregate and component.
