If your teammates were robots, leadership would be a lot easier. They would do what they're told, wouldn't get offended, and would methodically evaluate all options. But we don't get robots. We get people.

Real people have emotions. Real people take shortcuts. Real people work better when they're motivated. And, as invincible as you might feel, you're a real person too. Leadership skills include managing your own mindset.

  • Chapter 19 : Personal Mindset will teach us how to overcome the mental traps that can hold great PMs back. We'll learn about growth mindset, maturity, egolessness, and impostor syndrome.
  • Chapter 20 : Collaboration will teach us how to work well with teammates and partners. We'll learn about resolving conflicts, psychological safety, and personality differences.
  • Chapter 21 : Influencing without Authority is just what it sounds like. We'll learn stakeholder management and how to drive decisions.
  • Chapter 22 : Communication will teach us how to avoid miscommunication. We'll learn best practices for written conversation, live conversations, meetings, and presentations.
  • Chapter 23 : Motivation and Inspiration will teach us how to keep our teams motivated. We'll learn about intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation and what to do during crunch time.
  • Chapter 24 : Ownership Mentality will teach us how to take full responsibility for our teams and products. We'll learn about filling in the whitespace and contributing beyond your product team.
  • Chapter 25 : Mentoring will teach us how to mentor others. We'll learn about apprenticeship, sponsorship, and giving feedback.
  • Chapter 26 : Working with Other Departments will dive deep into working with designers, engineers, and executives. We'll learn what they care about and how to build trust with them.

The ideas in these chapters are highly influenced by the culture at US tech companies. Great leadership can look very different in other cultures. The book The Culture Map by Erin Meyer shines a light on cultural differences.
